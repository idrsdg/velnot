Why Privacy Matters for Meeting Recorders

Most meeting tools upload your audio to their servers. Here's why that's a problem.

☁️

Cloud Tools Are a Liability

Every meeting you record with Otter.ai, Fireflies, or Fathom is stored on their servers. Your confidential client calls, internal strategy, and sensitive discussions are in someone else's hands.

🤖

Bots Create Friction

Bot-based tools send a visible participant to your meetings. Clients notice. It triggers consent questions. In some industries and institutions, it's outright prohibited.

⚖️

GDPR & Compliance Risk

Sending meeting audio to US-based servers raises GDPR concerns for EU organizations. Local processing eliminates the data transfer problem entirely.

Frequently Asked Questions

Privacy-specific questions about Velnot.

Is Velnot GDPR compliant?

Velnot stores all recordings locally on your device. No audio is transferred to Velnot's servers. When using AI summarization with your own OpenAI API key, transcript text is sent to OpenAI — you should review OpenAI's data processing agreement for your use case. For fully offline operation, skip the AI summary step.

Where is my audio stored?

On your computer only. Velnot saves recordings locally. They never leave your machine unless you explicitly export them. You can delete them at any time.

Does Velnot work without internet?

Yes. Recording and local storage work completely offline. The AI transcription and summary steps require an internet connection to reach OpenAI's API, but these are optional.

Can my IT department approve this?

Velnot has no cloud backend to approve. The app runs entirely locally. The only external connection is to OpenAI's API for optional AI features — using your own API key. This makes it significantly easier to clear with IT and compliance teams compared to cloud-based tools.

Will other meeting participants know I'm recording?

No bot joins the call, so participants see nothing unusual on their end. Always check your local laws regarding consent for recording calls before using any meeting recorder.
